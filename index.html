Dig This Volleyball

mission statement:

dig this volleyball is a community committed to developing and promoting the sport of volleyball from entry level to advanced player. our goal is to provide an environment where we can support each other in the development of skills and sportsmanship, while providing a fun and nurturing place for players to reach their maximum playing potential.

Meet The Team

May

One thing May loves about volleyball is that this is a team sport. You can also play this game without having to be awesome in all the skills, everyone has a specialty!

She started Dig This Volleyball because of her passion for the sport. She loves supporting new players and enjoys competitive games when she’s not playing mom to her 3 kids.

Kathy

Kathy has been an organizer of open play and tournaments for volleyball and basketball for the last few years. She also actively volunteers for New Yorkers Sports Association, a well reported local sports organization in the Chinese community.

Since introduced to volleyball at the age of 13 years old, her commitment to advancing in the sport has never wavered. She has attended multiple volleyball camps and training clinics and strives to be the best player possible.

Kevin

Kevin got introduced to volleyball at a late age. He was able to learn the sport rapidly and qualified for varsity in high school.

Although the Men's Volleyball program at Queens College was cut short because of funding, his passion for the sport didn't stop him from playing. He frequently attended the women's volleyball team to practice with them.

Currently he plays recreational in our Open Play.

Tony

Assistant women’s volleyball coach for Queensborough Community College.

Assistant women’s volleyball coach of the Loyola University of New Orleans from 2006-2008.

Tony has been involved with volleyball as a player and a coach for the past 20 years. His career began at 14 years of age while at Hippocrates High School in Kos, Greece.

He also played a Semi-Pro league where the team won the Fourth National Championship, moving the team up to the Third National Division.

Brian

Brian was an assistant coach on the girl’s volleyball team for Francis Lewis High School, a 5 time city champion!

Brian got introduced to volleyball at the age of 14 years old and was immediate hooked. He played through out his high school years and college. He knows the drills and conditioning to make us all better players.
